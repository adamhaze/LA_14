For this subquestion, I investigated the distribution of milk production in 1970 and 2017(first and last years of the data) by calculating the median milk produced for each, and their difference. I then sent a modified dataset, with just those 2 years, through a permutation test, and calculated the difference in medians between two random groupings of the dataset with no year labels at each permutation. After graphing the results of the test, and comparing it to the actual difference in the medians before the test, I concluded that the year of production, doesnt have a huge effect on the distribution of of milk production. From the graph, it seems as if most of the random groupings in the permutation test had lower difference in median milk production, than the actual difference between the two years. However, because the results tended to be around the 60th percentile, it shows that the difference in medians from the permutation groups, were actually pretty well spread above and below the actual difference. This shows that even though the two years had a significant difference in median milk production, because the permutation test showed a pretty even distribution of differences around the actual difference it can be inferred that the year labels are fairly insignificant and the distribution of milk production isnt significantly different between 1970 and 2017.
